RESEARCH
Scalog
Distributed Systems Research
Spring, Fall 2019

Scalog is a scalable, total ordered, fault tolerant, distributed log. Its novel separation of ordering and persisting data allows high throughput and low latency compared to Corfu. Shards of f+1 data nodes accept client append and read requests and write to Raft ordering nodes; communication is made more efficient through batching, removing lulls in activity and optimizing load. Implemented in GoLang with Kubernetes in a team of 3. Contributed to the paper, which was accepted to NSDI'20.

Github Paper (release in February)
Derecho
Distributed Systems Research
Spring, Fall 2019

Derecho is a sharded, grouped, high throughput, low latency distributed system build on RDMA, where messages are directly written into the receiver's memory without an interrupt. Allowed dynamically resized memory regions for nodes and silenced errors in orchestrated node shutdowns.

Pedestrian Prediction with Mask R-CNN
Computer Vision Research with Prof. Hariharan
Fall 2018

Improved the prediction accuracy of pedestrians about to cross the road to further the safety of self-driving cars. Combined local environmental cues into the input for crossing prediction. Improved accuracy from 54% to 62%. Cues were detected with Mask R-CNN, trained to segment traffic signs, traffic lights, and vehicles. Used PyTorch for the neural network, SciKit for PCA and SVM, working in a team of 3.
